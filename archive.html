Oct 4 20, I have made a grave mistake

I forgot to get behind the scenes footage for the video assignment so here's the final product which i'm not super proud of because of the quality issue and the underlying explanation of momentum.

I would have preferred that momentum was explained as an abstract concept and not a mathy/sciency way because what matters to them is the idea behind it, not the formula. So something like, "A mass in motion is called a momentum" or even simpler, "A thing that's moving is momentum is momentum." would have been more helpful and easier to understand. However, I know it's a bit too late for me to say this because I was the one editing this and actively tried to not intervene much further in other roles. If the group thinks that it's good, then I won't say much. Even getting feedback from chak, he sees momentum as mass x velocity, conservation of momentum is total momentum same before and after collision... which i guess works in its own way but I'm not fond of that sort of understanding. It's purely because I believe that understanding things at a fundamental level is more important in most cases such that it influences the way we perceive the world positively. In this case, the takeaway for law students and others will be, momentum is mass velocity, and won't consider what other examples exhibit momentum. I tried to imply through my editing that momentum is much more general than the book and the pen and if you think about how the text is moving (assume it has some mass), then that also has momentum. The guy falling asleep also shows momentum, and much more.

Nonetheless, I disregarded these issues when I was working on this video because I think it's a good lesson for all of us to reflect on. I definitely could have improved on it by changing the video completely. Just because you can make it perfect doesn't mean you should. If the video was perfect and got full marks, then everyone won't learn to improve how to make it better. Is it fair to leave room for improvement even if it means to sacrifice grades? For me, again and again, grades don't mean anything to me. This idea stuck with me from my physics teacher. I didn't understand why back then until I graduated highschool. So that's cool.

09/26/20. Why not social media/make-a-website for this?

I completely forgot to mention in the video and in here why I chose not to make something like twitter or facebook, whatever social media. It's because I don't like social media. The idea of it is shallow, so it's not worth it. I won't make one even at the cost of not being able to interact with people. Besides, if you want to contact me, you know where to do so.

Also, I can do fancy stuff and completely personalise it. Yeah, I could go onto those websites like wix or wordpress, but then the domain would contain their respective url and that sucks. What also also sucks is that there'll be no sense of creation if I'm not making it from scratch. Using a template and stuff is lame, even if it's easier.

All in all, I prefer to have control over my stuff. With github, at least I get to do anything I want (except domain name 'cuz that requires purchasing or somehow finding a free one which I can't be bothered to do.)
